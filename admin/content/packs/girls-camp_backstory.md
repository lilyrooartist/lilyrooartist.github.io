## Core narrative seed
Girls Camp is about a friend of a friend whose place in an old girls-camp memory changed after she became he. The rules say he cannot go to girls camp anymore, but Lily's memory still has the old version of the scene stored somewhere tender and confusing.

The song is not arguing with who he is now. It is about the lag between language and memory: wanting to say the right thing, loving the person in the present, and still missing the time before the labels caught up.

That is why the chorus moves like a practice drill. The pronouns keep landing, then re-landing, as if the song is teaching itself how to be accurate without pretending the past vanished.

Inside Analog Myth, Girls Camp is the first place the album makes its larger theme explicit: you can lose access to a time, a room, a ritual, or a version of someone without wanting to take anything away from who they are now.

## Anecdote hooks
1. A friend of a friend was once part of a girls-camp memory, then became he, and the old rule no longer had a place for him.
2. The song lives in the effort to say "him" clearly while admitting that memory can arrive late.
3. "I can say him and still miss her" is the emotional hinge: not rejection, but grief for a time that cannot come back.
4. The bluesy roots feeling ties it back to Lily's earlier sound while the story moves into the Analog Myth question of who gets to define the past.
